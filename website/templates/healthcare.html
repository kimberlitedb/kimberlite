The healthcare compliance tax

If you're building a healthcare app on Postgres or MySQL, you're building five extra systems.

Audit trail. Postgres triggers, manual change tables, or a parallel event store. Auditors ask: "Show me every read of this patient's chart in the last 90 days." You scramble.

Tamper evidence. Hash a row, store the hash in another row, hope nobody runs UPDATE. Most teams skip this entirely until OCR knocks.

Per-tenant encryption. Application-layer envelope encryption with a separate KMS, careful not to leak keys across the trust boundary. One mistake leaks PHI cross-tenant.

Point-in-time queries. Temporal table extensions, slowly-changing-dimension joins, or "we keep nightly snapshots, sorry." Malpractice review takes weeks.

GDPR Article 17 erasure. A grep across every table, every backup, every replica — and the hope that you got them all. Most teams pray instead of proving.

Kimberlite ships all five in the core. The walkthrough below proves it.

What you get on day one

✓ Tamper-evident PHI access log. Every read and write recorded in an append-only, hash-chained event stream. audit.verifyChain() walks the SHA-256 chain server-side and tells you the exact event index of any tampering.
✓ Native time-travel queries. SELECT … AS OF TIMESTAMP '2025-08-12T14:30:00Z' reconstructs the chart at any point in the past. Malpractice review goes from weeks to seconds.
✓ GDPR Article 17 erasure orchestration. compliance.erasure.request(subject_id) auto-discovers every stream containing the subject and tracks per-stream completion with a requestId correlation id. International patients are covered.
✓ GDPR Article 6 consent-basis tracking. Wire-level consent records carry the lawful basis ("Consent", "Contract", "VitalInterest", "PublicInterest", "LegalObligation", "LegitimateInterests") and a justification string. Auditors ask "on what legal basis did you process this PHI?" — you query.
✓ Multi-tenant key isolation. Cryptographic boundaries per tenant. A clinic per tenant means one bug doesn't leak PHI cross-clinic.
✓ BAA-friendly architecture. Self-hosted, your deployment, your keys. The Business Associate Agreement is between you and your hosting provider; Kimberlite the OSS substrate stays out of your PHI flow.

What "HIPAA-aligned" means here

Kimberlite ships the cryptographic and architectural primitives that HIPAA Technical Safeguards demand. That is necessary, not sufficient.

What we have NOT done: third-party HIPAA attestation, Business Associate Agreement with covered entities, OCR audit. Those are v1.0 gates.

What this means for you: a healthcare startup can build on Kimberlite today knowing the substrate is sound. The audit letter and the BAA are work your team (or we, post-v1.0) still have to do — BAA with your hosting provider, internal controls, pen test, audit firm. Kimberlite enables your HIPAA posture; certification remains your team's responsibility.
