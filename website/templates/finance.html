The fintech compliance tax

SEC 17a-4 alone demands four things most databases don't ship.

Non-rewriteable, non-erasable storage. WORM media, blockchain anchoring, or "we just promise nobody runs UPDATE." Most teams skip the proof and pray.

3-6 year retention with immediate availability. Cold-storage Glacier dumps that take 12 hours to restore. SOX auditor asks for end-of-quarter Q3 2022 — your team scrambles for a week.

Complete audit trail of every access. Postgres triggers, manual log tables, or a parallel SIEM. One missing trigger and OCC's enforcement division knocks.

Tamper evidence on a per-row basis. Hash a row, store the hash elsewhere, hope the hash table doesn't get tampered with. Most teams ship without this and find out at the broker-dealer audit.

Kimberlite ships all four in the core. The walkthrough below proves it.

What you get on day one

✓ Append-only ledger by construction. Every trade, journal entry, KYC update is hash-chained into an immutable log. UPDATE and DELETE exist for projection-table semantics; the underlying event log is non-rewriteable.
✓ Point-in-time balance reconciliation. SELECT … AS OF TIMESTAMP '2024-06-30T23:59:59Z' reconstructs the book at end-of-quarter. Q3 audit goes from a week of grep to a single query.
✓ Server-walked audit-chain attestation. audit.verifyChain() walks the SHA-256 chain server-side and tells you the exact event index of any tampering. SOX §404 internal-controls proof, on demand.
✓ GDPR Article 6 lawful-basis tracking. Wire-level consent records carry the basis ("Contract" for KYC, "LegalObligation" for AML, "LegitimateInterests" for fraud detection) so EU regulators can see the legal grounding for every customer record.
✓ PCI DSS scope reduction via column masking. CREATE MASKING POLICY composes with RBAC + break-glass. Auditor reviews the role; cardholder data never leaves the cryptographic boundary.
✓ Multi-tenant cryptographic isolation. One book per tenant, separate AES-256-GCM keys. Cross-tenant leaks aren't a code-review thing — they're physically impossible.

What "SEC / SOX / GLBA-aligned" means here

Kimberlite ships the cryptographic and architectural primitives that these frameworks demand. That is necessary, not sufficient.

What we have NOT done: third-party SOC 2 Type II audit, broker-dealer registration, PCI DSS Level 1 attestation. Those are v1.0 gates.

What this means for you: a fintech can build on Kimberlite today knowing the substrate is sound. The SOC 2 audit, the FINRA registration, the PCI scope-reduction work are still your team's job — internal controls, pen test, audit firm engagement. Kimberlite enables your compliance posture; certification remains your team's responsibility.
